# 质量检查清单

Use this before delivering a draft or revision.

## Political and institutional stance

- Does the draft respect the sender/recipient hierarchy?
- Are policy statements accurate and not fabricated?
- Are terms such as `党委`, `政府`, `党组`, `机关`, `单位`, `部门` used correctly?
- Is sensitive or uncertain content marked for confirmation?

## Document type fit

- `请示`: asks for approval and has one main request.
- `报告`: informs and does not request approval.
- `通知`: assigns or announces work.
- `函`: coordinates with equal/non-subordinate parties.
- `纪要`: records agreed matters rather than rewriting speeches.

## Structure and logic

- Does the first paragraph explain why the document exists?
- Do headings follow one logic dimension instead of mixing time, task, and department?
- Are tasks matched with responsible parties, deadlines, and expected results where available?
- Are achievements, problems, and measures separated clearly?

## Language

- Prefer concrete official verbs over vague praise.
- Remove repeated phrases such as `高度重视`, `扎实推进`, `取得实效` when unsupported.
- Avoid internet slang, excessive emotion, and ornamental metaphors.
- Keep punctuation and numbering consistent.

## Fact safety

- Do not invent data, names, citations, meetings, dates, or approvals.
- Preserve all user-supplied facts unless the user asks to rewrite them.
- Mark missing facts with square-bracket placeholders.

## Delivery

- Provide a clean draft first.
- Add a short `待确认事项` section only when needed.
- If the user asked for polishing, explain the main edits briefly.
