# 法定公文类型参考

Use this file when the task is a formal official document.

## 通知

Purpose: arrange work, convey requirements, announce matters.

Structure:

1. Title: `关于...的通知`
2. Main sending units.
3. Basis/background: why this notice is issued.
4. Work items: numbered tasks or arrangements.
5. Requirements: responsibility, deadline, reporting, supervision.
6. Closing: `请认真贯彻执行。` or similar.

Watch for:

- Tasks must have responsible subjects.
- Avoid requesting approval from recipients; a notice commands or arranges.

## 报告

Purpose: report work, situation, problems, or suggestions to a superior without requesting approval.

Structure:

1. Title: `关于...情况的报告`
2. Background or trigger.
3. Main work/situation.
4. Problems and causes.
5. Next steps or suggestions.
6. Closing: `特此报告。`

Watch for:

- Do not end with `妥否，请批示` unless it is actually a request.

## 请示

Purpose: request approval, instruction, funding, authorization, or coordination from a superior.

Structure:

1. Title: `关于...的请示`
2. Reason and necessity.
3. Basis and feasibility.
4. One clear requested matter.
5. Proposed implementation.
6. Closing: `妥否，请批示。`

Watch for:

- One document should generally contain one main request.
- Do not send to multiple superior authorities when a single approval chain is required.

## 通报

Purpose: praise, criticize, or communicate important situations.

Structure:

1. Facts.
2. Assessment or decision.
3. Lessons or implications.
4. Work requirements.

Tone:

- Objective facts first.
- Praise or criticism should be proportionate and supported.

## 函

Purpose: communicate between equal or non-subordinate organizations.

Structure:

1. Matter and background.
2. Requested coordination or reply.
3. Time or material requirements.
4. Closing: `专此函达，请予支持。` or `盼复。`

Tone:

- Polite, clear, non-commanding unless authorized.

## 会议纪要

Purpose: record agreed matters, responsibilities, and implementation requirements.

Structure:

1. Meeting time, host, attendees, topic.
2. Meeting consensus.
3. Agreed tasks.
4. Responsible units and deadlines.
5. Follow-up requirements.
